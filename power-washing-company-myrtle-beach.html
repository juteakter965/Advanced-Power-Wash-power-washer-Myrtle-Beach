Salt Air Corrosion and Deposit Removal

Salt spray from the Atlantic Ocean deposits corrosive salt crystals on all exterior surfaces within miles of the coastline. These salt deposits attract moisture, accelerating corrosion of metal fixtures, deterioration of painted surfaces, and degradation of building materials. Regular professional power washing removes accumulated salt deposits before they cause permanent damage.

Professional salt removal requires more than simple pressure washing. Quality companies pre-treat salt-affected areas with salt-neutralizing solutions breaking down embedded salt crystals before pressure washing. Thorough rinsing proves critical—residual salt promotes rapid reaccumulation and continued damage. Professional services use high water volume (3.0+ GPM) ensuring complete salt removal from porous surfaces like stucco and brick.

Algae, Mold, and Mildew Treatment

Myrtle Beach's humid climate with average humidity exceeding 70% promotes aggressive algae, mold, and mildew growth on all exterior surfaces. Shaded areas, north-facing walls, and locations with limited airflow develop visible organic growth within months of cleaning without proper treatment.

Professional organic growth removal combines appropriate chemicals with proper pressure washing techniques. Quality companies apply professional-grade algaecides and mildewcides killing growth at root level, allow sufficient dwell time for chemical penetration (15-20 minutes), then pressure wash thoroughly. This chemical treatment prevents regrowth for 12-24 months versus 3-6 months from pressure-only cleaning. Many companies offer preventive treatment applications extending results even further.

Hurricane and Storm Preparation

Coastal properties benefit from pre-season power washing before hurricane season (June-November) removing loose debris, organic growth, and deposits that become airborne projectiles during storms. Clean exterior surfaces also facilitate post-storm damage assessment and insurance documentation. Many Myrtle Beach residents schedule annual spring power washing preparing properties for active hurricane season while addressing accumulated winter deterioration.

What's the difference between pressure washing and soft washing?

Pressure washing and soft washing represent fundamentally different cleaning approaches suited for different applications. Pressure washing uses high-pressure water streams (1,500-4,000+ PSI) to physically remove dirt, stains, and contaminants through mechanical force, effective for durable hard surfaces including concrete driveways, walkways, brick, stone, and properly-prepared wood decks. Pressure washing excels at removing embedded salt deposits, heavy staining, oil spots, and stubborn debris from surfaces tolerating aggressive mechanical cleaning. Soft washing combines low pressure (under 500 PSI, similar to garden hose pressure) with specialized cleaning solutions to chemically kill and remove organic growth including algae, mold, mildew, and lichen. Soft washing proves superior for delicate surfaces easily damaged by high pressure including roof shingles, painted surfaces, stucco, vinyl siding, older wood surfaces, and decorative architectural features. The chemical treatment kills organic growth at root level providing significantly longer-lasting results (18-24 months) versus pressure-only cleaning (6-9 months) that temporarily blasts growth away without killing roots. Professional power washing companies employ both techniques appropriately: soft washing for roofs, painted surfaces, and organic growth treatment; pressure washing for driveways, concrete, and heavy stain removal from durable surfaces. Comprehensive property cleaning typically requires both approaches—soft washing home exterior and roof, pressure washing driveway and walkways. Quality companies understand which technique suits each specific surface type and condition, preventing damage while delivering effective lasting results.

How do I maintain my property between professional power washing services?

Simple maintenance between professional services extends results significantly while reducing required cleaning frequency. Conduct monthly quick inspections walking property perimeter identifying developing algae spots, spider webs, dirt accumulation, and staining before they become extensive problems requiring professional treatment. Address visible spots promptly with garden hose rinsing or mild detergent application—early intervention prevents extensive growth. Maintain clean gutters preventing overflow that stains siding and promotes mildew growth; ensure proper drainage directing water away from foundations and exterior walls since poor drainage creates constant moisture accelerating organic growth. Trim vegetation maintaining 12-18 inches clearance from structures—plants touching siding hold moisture promoting mildew while leaves and debris create organic staining, proper landscape management dramatically reduces cleaning requirements. Remove spider webs, wasp nests, and insect debris regularly using long-handled broom or cobweb brush before they accumulate significantly. Address stains from rust, oil, bird droppings, and other sources promptly before they set permanently—many stains remove easily when fresh but require professional treatment after sitting weeks or months. Rinse high-traffic areas including entrances, garage doors, and walkways monthly with garden hose removing accumulating dirt before it becomes embedded. For properties within 3 miles of ocean, periodic fresh water rinsing after storms removes salt spray before it accumulates excessively. Consider applying preventive treatments—many professional companies offer algaecide/mildewcide applications extending results 6-12 months beyond standard cleaning. Schedule regular professional comprehensive cleaning annually or bi-annually depending on property location and conditions, then perform simple maintenance between professional services. This balanced approach maintains consistently clean appearance while maximizing value from professional service investments and minimizing total property maintenance costs and effort over time.
